üìù The German Noun System: Gender, Case, Declension

German nouns have 3 genders (Der, Die, Das), 4 cases (Nominative, Accusative, Dative, Genitive), and plurals. This is your C1 foundation.

4 Cases of German

üìå Remember: The case tells you the function of the noun in the sentence. Nominative = subject. Accusative = direct object. Dative = indirect object. Genitive = possession.

NOMINATIVE (Nominativ) - Subject / Predicate

Function: Who or what is doing the action?

ACCUSATIVE (Akkusativ) - Direct Object

Function: Who or what is receiving the action directly?

Ich sehe den Mann.
I see the man. [Man = direct object]

DATIVE (Dativ) - Indirect Object

Function: To whom or for whom? After certain prepositions.

Ich gebe dem Lehrer einen Kaffee.

Das Buch liegt neben dem Fenster.

GENITIVE (Genitiv) - Possession

Function: Who does it belong to? (The "of" case)

Das ist das Buch des Lehrers.
That is the book of the teacher. [Possession]
